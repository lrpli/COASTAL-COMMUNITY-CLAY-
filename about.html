About

Built by artists, powered by community.

Coastal Community Clay supports ceramic education, residencies, and public programming for the central Oregon coast.

Our mission

Coastal Community Clay is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit ceramic arts organization dedicated to making clay education accessible to everyone on the central Oregon coast. We exist to reduce financial barriers to the arts, preserve coastal craft traditions, and build a creative community rooted in the ecology and culture of our region.

Our nonprofit model keeps tuition affordable through sliding-scale pricing, expands access through need-based scholarships, and sustains working artists who live and teach on the coast. Every membership fee, donation, and grant dollar is reinvested directly into programs, equipment, and people.

Community-first

Sliding scale tuition, open studio hours, and public events free to all.

Artist-led

Programs shaped and led by resident artists, visiting instructors, and peers.

Coastal focus

We celebrate coastal ecology, indigenous story, and ocean-inspired design.

Our history

2019

Founded

Coastal Community Clay was established in Yachats by a founding group of six coastal potters who saw a gap in affordable arts education on the central Oregon coast. Operating initially out of a rented garage with two wheels and a single electric kiln, the studio held its first six-week class with 12 participants.

2020

501(c)(3) status granted

The organization received federal nonprofit recognition, opening the door to grant funding and tax-deductible donations. A small emergency arts grant helped sustain operations and launch the first remote youth clay kits program during studio closures.

2021

Permanent studio space

CCC secured its current location at 286 NE Evergreen Ln, Yachats — a renovated building with space for 12 pottery wheels, a glaze lab, and a wood-fire kiln yard. Membership grew to over 60 active members within the first year.

2022

School partnership program launched

We formalized partnerships with four Lincoln County schools, bringing traveling clay labs to classrooms from Waldport to Lincoln City. More than 200 students worked with clay for the first time in that inaugural year.

2023

Community wood kiln completed

Volunteers spent eight months constructing the studio's Anagama-style community wood kiln — the first of its kind open to the public on the central coast. The inaugural firing included works from 34 participants across three firing days.

2024–present

Expanding access

Today CCC serves more than 120 active members, awards 36 scholarships annually, and maintains partnerships with 14 schools and cultural organizations. We are currently raising funds for a dedicated youth ceramics room and expanded kiln infrastructure.

Active projects

These are the current initiatives driving our work in 2025–2026.

Youth

Youth Clay Lab

A dedicated after-school ceramics program serving students ages 8–18 from Yachats, Waldport, and Seal Rock. Participants complete six-week intensives, culminating in a public showcase each spring. Full scholarships available for all enrolled students.

180 youth served since launch

Community

Community Wood Kiln Program

Three to four times per year, CCC opens its Anagama wood kiln to the community for multi-day firings. Any member or partner school student may submit one bisque piece per firing. The project builds technical skills and shared stewardship of a rare coastal resource.

320+ community pieces fired to date

Residency

Coastal Artist Residency

Each year CCC hosts two four-month resident artists, providing free studio access, a small stipend, and public programming requirements. Residents lead workshops, contribute to the community market, and mentor junior members. Applications open each September.

8 residents supported since 2022

Schools

Traveling Clay Labs

CCC staff bring portable clay equipment directly to Lincoln County classrooms, delivering four-session ceramic units aligned with Oregon arts standards. Schools receive all materials free of charge through our grant-funded supply fund.

14 school partners, 500+ students reached

Access

Scholarship Fund

Need-based scholarships cover full tuition for any class or workshop. Funded primarily through our annual Coastal Clay Market and individual donations, the scholarship fund has grown each year since its founding.

36 scholarships awarded annually

Capital

Youth Studio Expansion

We are currently raising $48,000 to build a dedicated youth ceramics room with smaller-scale equipment, adjustable worktables, and a separate glaze station. Construction is planned for late 2026, pending full funding.

Capital campaign in progress

Leadership

Our board and staff are makers, educators, and community volunteers. We partner with local schools, libraries, tribal cultural organizations, and county arts programs to widen access to ceramic arts on the coast.
